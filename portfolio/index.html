Overview

+SABZ (Mosbat Sabz) is an online shopping platform specializing in cosmetic and pharmaceutical supplement sales. As the business scaled, the legacy website could no longer meet modern usability and scalability needs. My responsibility was to lead the redesign of the entire website.

Problem
  • Complex navigation for users searching by health condition
  • Outdated interface inconsistent with the brand's new identity
  • Poor mobile experience leading to high bounce rates
  • Long 4-step checkout process increasing cart abandonment
Research
  • Google Analytics: 60% of users left during checkout
  • Competitor analysis: iHerb, Sephora best practices
  • Two personas: Sara (32, health-conscious) & Nima (27, fitness-oriented)
Wireframes & User Flows

Detailed user flows and roadmaps designed in Miro, simplifying checkout from 4 steps to 2.

UI Design — Desktop & Mobile

The home page was designed in Figma following flat design principles and the brand's color palette. Based on the MVP approach, non-essential content was removed to focus on core functionalities.

Research Deep-Dive
Solution & Process
  • Clean UI system with consistent grid and typography hierarchy
  • Simplified navigation: Shop by Category, Health Needs, Brands, Offers
  • Two-step checkout replacing the previous four-step process
  • Trust elements: verified badges, ratings, clear delivery info
Results
  • 40% reduction in cart abandonment rate
  • Improved mobile conversion rate after responsive optimization
  • Higher user retention due to simplified navigation
  • All steps documented in an 80-page web design report

Mosbat Sabz — Admin Panel & Warehouse App
Senior Product Designer · Feb 2022 – Sep 2024 · Tools: Figma, Adobe XD, Google Analytics
Visit mosbatesabz.com
Overview

Mosbat Sabz needed a comprehensive admin panel and a custom warehouse app to streamline order collection and dispatch across multiple warehouse zones (cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and supplements).

Problem & Research

Before this redesign, Mosbat Sabz's admin system lacked a unified structure. Most customer orders included items from at least two warehouses, all needing to be packed into a single shipment with zero tolerance for error.

Solution & Process
Warehouse App
  • Warehouse separation in the database for better routing
  • User-based task visibility for each employee
  • Barcode system for faster, accurate picking
  • Partial fulfillment prevention
Admin Panel
  • Admin Panel Design and Implementation
  • Shop Site Redesign and Implementation
  • Warehouse Device Design and Implementation
  • 32-page brand guidelines document
UI Screens
Warehouse App Design
Results & Reflection
  • Improved visual consistency across the digital ecosystem
  • Internal team adopted the new system smoothly thanks to clear documentation
  • Warehouse operations became faster and more accurate
